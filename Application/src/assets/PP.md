## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how LibreChat (“LibreChat”, “we”, “us”, or “our”), operated under the name RedFolder, collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data when you use our services.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), RedFolder acts as the data controller for personal data processed through its core backend services.

It is important that you read this entire policy. The following is a brief summary:

---

LibreChat is designed to minimize data collection and maximize user control. Many parts of the system rely on local storage, encrypted communication, and independently hosted servers.

---

### Limited data collection

We only collect the data necessary to:

* create and manage accounts

* maintain security and reliability

Additional data may be optional or dependent on how you use the service.

This means:

* we do not have access to the content of encrypted messages

---

### Decentralized environment

These servers:

* are not controlled by RedFolder
* may process data independently

You are responsible for choosing which environments you trust.

---

### Limited moderation and visibility

Due to encryption and system design:

* we do not have access to the content of encrypted messages
* we may not be able to monitor, review, or moderate user activity

---

### User control

You have control over:

* the data you provide

* the content you share

However, some data may not be recoverable if lost or stored outside our control.

---

### No sale of personal data

We do not sell your personal data.

LibreChat is designed to minimize data collection and avoid business models based on personal data exploitation. This principle is a core part of how the service is built.

## 2. About LibreChat

LibreChat is a communication platform designed to give users more control over how they connect, share, and interact.

It supports direct messages, group conversations, and server-based environments, while allowing a high level of customization and flexibility in how those spaces are structured and used.

Unlike traditional platforms, LibreChat is built around a hybrid model:

* parts of the system run locally on your device
* communication may be end-to-end encrypted
* servers can be independently hosted and managed

Because of this, LibreChat does not function as a fully centralized platform.

---

Users can choose where and how they communicate. Depending on the environment:

* conversations may be private or shared
* access may be restricted or public
* server operators may define their own rules and permissions

You should always be aware of where you are sharing content and who may have access to it.

---

LibreChat is designed with a focus on flexibility and privacy rather than centralized control.

This means:

* we do not have access to the content of encrypted messages
* we do not operate a central moderation system across all environments
* we rely on users and server operators to manage their own spaces

---

LibreChat is an independently developed project.

It is built with the intention of providing a more transparent and user-controlled alternative to traditional communication platforms, while keeping data collection minimal and avoiding business models based on personal data exploitation.
